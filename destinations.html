Discover Kashmir’s Most Beautiful Destinations

Every valley, lake, and meadow tells a story.

Srinagar

Srinagar is the cultural and emotional heart of Kashmir, built around the timeless Dal Lake and framed by the Zabarwan hills.

From early morning Shikara rides to evenings in Mughal gardens, Srinagar blends history, craftsmanship, cuisine, and daily life. Houseboats tell stories of a bygone era while local markets reveal authentic Kashmiri culture.

Gulmarg

Gulmarg, meaning “Meadow of Flowers,” is Kashmir’s most iconic hill destination, known for its alpine beauty and adventure experiences.

In winter, Gulmarg transforms into India’s premier skiing destination, attracting snow lovers from across the world. During summer, its vast green meadows bloom with wildflowers, offering breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks. The Gulmarg Gondola, one of the highest cable cars in the world, provides panoramic vistas that leave travelers speechless. Whether you seek thrill or tranquility, Gulmarg delivers both with ease.

Pahalgam

Set along the gentle Lidder River, Pahalgam is a serene retreat surrounded by pine forests, meadows, and mountain air.

Pahalgam is perfect for travelers who enjoy slow, peaceful journeys. Horse rides through Baisaran, riverside walks, and scenic viewpoints make it ideal for families and honeymooners alike. As a base for the Amarnath Yatra, the town holds spiritual significance, yet it remains calm and unhurried. Pahalgam invites you to disconnect from chaos and reconnect with nature.

Sonmarg

Sonmarg, the “Meadow of Gold,” is a high-altitude valley famous for its glaciers, alpine landscapes, and untouched beauty.

Surrounded by towering mountains and flowing streams, Sonmarg feels raw and majestic. It serves as the gateway to several Himalayan treks and offers pony rides to glaciers during summer. The changing light across its meadows creates dramatic scenery, making it a favorite among photographers and nature lovers. Sonmarg is ideal for those who want to experience Kashmir in its most pristine form.

Doodhpathri

Doodhpathri is one of Kashmir’s lesser-known gems, celebrated for its rolling green meadows and peaceful atmosphere.

Translating to “Valley of Milk,” Doodhpathri is known for its milky-white streams flowing through lush grasslands. Free from heavy tourist crowds, it offers a quiet escape for travelers who value solitude and open landscapes. Picnics, photography, and leisurely walks are the essence of this destination. Doodhpathri showcases Kashmir’s softer, gentler side.

Gurez Valley

Gurez Valley is a remote and culturally rich region, offering untouched landscapes and a glimpse into traditional Kashmiri life.

Nestled near the Line of Control, Gurez remains largely unexplored, preserving its raw charm and authenticity. Snow-capped peaks, wooden houses, and friendly locals create a timeless atmosphere. Traveling to Gurez feels like stepping back in time, far from commercialization. It is ideal for explorers seeking authenticity, culture, and solitude.

Yusmarg

Yusmarg is a tranquil meadow destination known for its wide-open spaces, pine-covered hills, and breathtaking mountain views.

Unlike busier tourist spots, Yusmarg offers quiet trails, gentle slopes, and panoramic vistas of the Himalayas. It’s perfect for short hikes, picnics, and travelers who prefer calm, uncrowded environments. The simplicity of Yusmarg allows visitors to fully absorb Kashmir’s natural beauty without distractions.
